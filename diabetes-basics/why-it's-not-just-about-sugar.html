Why it’s not just about sugar

Diabetes Basics: Sugar

You may think that if you have diabetes, you should cut sugar from your diet. In fact, some people believe eating too much sugar causes diabetes.

The facts:

Neither is true.

  • Factors like genetics, excess weight, high blood pressure and high cholesterol all raise the risk of Type 2 diabetes.
  • Type 1 diabetes runs in families. Other triggers, such as environmental factors, may contribute to its development.

It’s about more than sweets

There are many ways to stay healthy with diabetes.

  • Eat a nutritious, balanced diet. Follow Canada’s Food Guide.
  • Stay active with regular exercise. Build up to 150 minutes per week.
  • Keep blood pressure within a healthy range. Nutritious eating, exercise and even medication could make a difference.
  • Keep cholesterol low. Eat foods low in saturated and trans fats, and talk to your doctor about medication, if necessary.
  • If you smoke, quit. Call the Smoker’s Help Line toll-free support line: 1-866-366-3667, and visit quitsmoking.shoppersdrugmart.ca to get started.
  • Talk to your health care provider to learn about diabetes and how to live with it.

Enjoy sugar responsibly

Having diabetes doesn’t mean never having sugar again. Many foods that are high in natural sugar, like fruit and milk, are an important part of a healthy diet. Here are some helpful tips for eating foods with sugar:

  • Stick to small portions of sugary foods. You should be filling up on foods that are nutritious, not empty of vitamins and minerals.

  • To keep blood sugar from spiking, don’t eat a sweet treat on its own, but as part of a complete meal.
  • Once you’re eating better and exercising more often, it will be easier to lose extra pounds. And at a healthy weight, it will be easier to reach blood pressure, cholesterol and blood sugar goals.
