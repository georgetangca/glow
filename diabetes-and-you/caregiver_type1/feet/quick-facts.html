About 50 per cent of people with diabetes have neuropathy--a type of nerve damage--and 15 per cent of Canadians with diabetes will end up dealing with a serious foot sore, such as a foot ulcer.

Quick Facts: feet

There are four types of neuropathy that affect people with diabetes. The most common--peripheral neuropathy--can cause pain or numbness in the feet (which can be worse at night), and problems with balance and coordination.

Your loved one will be more likely to have neuropathy if:

  • Their blood sugar is not controlled
  • They have high cholesterol
  • They have high blood pressure
  • They are overweight

What happens when they can’t feel their feet?

  • They might not realize they have a blister or a sore. Be sure to check their feet regularly.
  • An injury could become infected if it isn’t treated, so ensure they seek medical attention.

Nerve compression

  • The nerves in people with diabetes are often swollen and inflamed. As a result, they can become compressed.

What you can do

Make sure they get regular physical examinations, and check their daily for any skin abnormalities or changes.

Did you know?

Physical exercise helps their feet! Being active boosts circulation.
