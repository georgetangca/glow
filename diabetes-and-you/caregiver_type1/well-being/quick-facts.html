Getting diagnosed with diabetes can cause anxiety and stress.

Quick Facts: Well-being

What we know:

  • Trying to manage diabetes can be stressful.
  • When complications from diabetes affect quality of life, it can lead to feelings of sadness.

Chemical considerations

  • High levels of stress hormones over a period of time can cause high blood sugar levels.
  • Stress hormones can make insulin less effective.

What to watch for

  • If you are caring for someone with diabetes who may be having trouble coping, ensure that they take their medications properly. Your pharmacist can help you do that. Research shows people with diabetes are less likely to properly take their medications and look after their health if they have a mental health condition.

What you can do:

  • Make sure their daily routine includes physical activity.
  • Teach them relaxation techniques, such as deep breathing exercises.
  • Help them practise positive thinking.
  • Get them into the community by encouraging them to join a club, take lessons or volunteer.
  • Introduce them to a diabetes support group and a health care professional that specializes in day-to-day diabetes management.
