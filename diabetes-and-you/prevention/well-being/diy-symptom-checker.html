While diabetes can lead to depression, it can also work the other way around. Depression can lead to diabetes because of poor health choices, including a lack of motivation to maintain good health.

DIY wellness symptom checker

“People who are depressed may have a tougher time monitoring their sugars and doing the appropriate exercise and eating healthily… so that can be a risk factor for ongoing problems with disease management,” says Dr. Michelle Haring, consultant for the Canadian Mental Health Association and director of the North Shore Stress and Anxiety Clinic in North Vancouver. Take our symptom-checker quiz.

  • Do you feel abnormal levels of fatigue?
  • Are you edgy or irritable?
  • Do you have difficulty concentrating?
  • Have you lost interest in hobbies or activities you normally enjoy?
  • Do you generally feel down or sad?

When to see a doctor:

“If [any of the above] symptoms are persistent and if they start impacting your ability to function, that’s the point at which you need to go to your physician to be assessed,” says Dr. Haring.
