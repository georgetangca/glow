Diabetes is the number one cause of blindness in adults under the age of 50 in Canada. Yet many people don't realize they have diabetes or recognize that is causing damage, until it's too late.

Quick Facts: Eyes

Diabetic retinopathy is a condition in which tiny blood vessels in the eye are damaged, or leak, from the effects of high blood sugar.

  • It's the most common health complication of diabetes.
  • It affects half a million Canadians.
  • One fifth of people with diabetic retinopathy have a severe form that destroys vision.
  • 60 per cent of people with Type 2 diabetes will have diabetic retinopathy in their first 20 years of living with diabetes.

What other eye diseases are people at risk for when they have diabetes?

40 per cent higher risk of glaucoma

  • The risk rises with age and the length of time a person has had diabetes.

60 per cent higher risk of cataracts

  • People with diabetes develop cataracts earlier and faster.

  • Smoking and untreated high blood pressure can worsen eye problems from diabetes. For tips on quitting smoking for good visit quitsmoking.shoppersdrugmart.ca

The case for an eye exam

  • Half of all Canadians who don’t wear glasses are not getting regular eye exams.
  • Whether you are living with diabetes or not, have a regular eye exam to check for damage. Once a year should do the trick.
  • You may have no eye symptoms, yet early treatment can save your vision.
  • Up to a fifth of people with Type 2 diabetes already have eye damage from the disease when they are diagnosed.
