About 50 per cent of people with diabetes have neuropathy – damage to the nerves – and 15 per cent of Canadians with diabetes will end up dealing with a serious foot sore, such as a foot ulcer.

Quick Facts: feet

There are four types of neuropathy that affect people with diabetes. The most common, peripheral neuropathy, can cause pain or numbness in the feet (which can be worse at night), and problems with balance and coordination.

You are more likely to have neuropathy if:

  • Your blood sugar is not controlled
  • You have high cholesterol
  • You have high blood pressure
  • You are overweight

What happens when you can’t feel your feet?

  • You might not realize you have a blister or a sore.
  • An injury could become infected if it isn’t treated.

Nerve compression

  • The nerves in people with diabetes are often swollen and inflamed. As a result, they can become compressed.

What you can do:

Cuts and bruises that take a long time to heal can be a silent symptom of diabetes. Be sure to talk to your doctor about this.

Did you know?

Physical exercise helps your feet! Being active boosts circulation.
