Keeping consistent with exercise, such as walking, is a good way to help lower your risk of diabetes while managing your weight.

Walking Workouts for Preventing Diabetes

Try one of these walking workouts by Amanda Vogel, MA human kinetics. (Be sure to get clearance from your doctor to exercise.)

What’s your BMI? The Body Mass Index provides a weight-to-height ratio, in order to gauge whether you are at risk for health issues or diseases. To find out more, click here: http://www1.shoppersdrugmart.ca/en/Health-And-Pharmacy/Health-Tools/BMICalculator.aspx

Beginner Walking Workout (30 minutes):

Try this workout if you haven’t exercised in the past three months or more. Start with two days a week, working up to three days a week.

First 7 minutes: Walk at an easy pace to warm up. You should be able to speak comfortably.

Next 18 minutes: Increase to a moderately brisk pace, pumping your arms and widening your stride. Your breathing should get deeper. You can speak in short sentences but not as easily as during the warm-up.

Final 5 minutes: Cool down with an easy walk. End with stretching all major muscles.

Intermediate Walking Workout (40 minutes):

Go for this workout if you’ve been walking for at least three months and you’re ready to try a greater challenge. Start with three times a week, working up to four.

First 5 minutes: Walk at an easy pace to warm up. You should be able to speak comfortably.

Next 15 minutes: Increase to a brisk pace, pumping your arms and widening your stride. Your breathing should get deeper. You can speak in short sentences, but not as easily as during the warm-up.

Next 10 minutes: Increase your pace slightly for about 30 seconds by speeding up, or, if you’re using a treadmill, set it to a slight incline. You should be breathing deeply and only able to speak a few words at a time by the end of the 30 seconds.

Walk at a moderate pace for one minute, then try another 30-second interval at a slightly faster pace or if using a treadmill set it at a higher incline. Repeat this pattern until the 10 minutes are up.

Next 5 minutes: Go back to a brisk pace.

Final 5 minutes: Cool down with an easy walk. End with stretching all major muscles.

If you’re not partial to walks, you can always join a group fitness class or athletic team at your local community centre.
