Getting diagnosed with diabetes can cause anxiety and stress.

Quick Facts: Well-being

What we know:

  • Trying to manage diabetes can be stressful.
  • When complications from diabetes affect quality of life, it can lead to feelings of sadness.

Chemical considerations

  • High levels of stress hormones over a period of time can cause high blood sugar levels.
  • Stress hormones can make insulin less effective.

Why seek help?

  • If you're having trouble coping, ensure that you are taking your medications properly. Your pharmacist can help. Research shows that people with diabetes are less likely to properly take their medications and look after their health if they have a mental health condition.

What you can do:

  • Make sure your daily routine includes physical activity.
  • Learn relaxation techniques, such as deep breathing exercises.
  • Practise positive thinking.
  • Find a way to be around other people: Try joining a club, taking lessons or volunteering.
  • Join a diabetes support group and talk to your health care professional to get tips on managing your diabetes.
