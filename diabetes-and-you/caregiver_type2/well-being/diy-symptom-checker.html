Life can be difficult for people with diabetes and those caring for them.

DIY wellness symptom checker

"[Once diagnosed], there are all these changes they need to make to their life, to their diet, to their exercise," says Dr. Michelle Haring, consultant for the Canadian Mental Health Association, and director of the North Shore Stress and Anxiety Clinic in North Vancouver. Frustration, anger, even depression, can come along with diabetes. You will need to watch out for that. "People who are depressed may have a tougher time monitoring their sugars and doing the appropriate exercise and eating healthily, so that can be a risk factor for ongoing problems with disease management." Take our symptom-checker quiz.

  • Do they feel abnormal levels of fatigue?
  • Are they edgy or irritable?
  • Do they have difficulty concentrating?
  • Have they lost interest in hobbies or activities they normally enjoy?
  • Do they generally feel down or sad?

When to see a doctor:

"If [any of the above] symptoms are persistent and if they start impacting their ability to function, that's the point at which they need to go to their physician to be assessed," says Dr. Haring.
