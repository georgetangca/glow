People with diabetes are at risk of developing diabetic retinopathy or open-angle glaucoma, and can get cataracts at a younger age than those who don’t have diabetes.

DIY Symptom Checker: Eyes

"Sixty per cent of patients with Type 2 diabetes have some kind of retinopathy during the first 20 years they have diabetes," says Dr. Keith Gordon, vice-president research and service quality, at CNIB. He notes that both open-angle glaucoma and diabetic retinopathy are often symptomless, so it's important to test your eyes regularly, even if your sight appears to be normal. Any changes to your vision could indicate the disease has advanced. Take our symptom-checker quiz.

Diabetic Retinopathy

  • Do you see spots, dots or blobs in your field of vision?
  • Do you have double vision?
  • Is your vision blurry?
  • Have you experienced vision loss?

Open-Angle Glaucoma

  • Do you have difficulty seeing in your peripheral field?

Cataracts

  • Do you see halos?
  • Do you have double vision?

When to see a doctor:

If you answer "yes" to any of these questions, get your eyes checked. "I encourage people to get regular eye exams—once a year or more often, if possible—especially if they have diabetes," says Dr. Gordon. If you experience any changes to your vision, see your eye doctor immediately. Dr. Gordon adds that there are treatments and drugs available to help control the diseases, so he advises asking your ophthalmologist about them if you have eye disease.
